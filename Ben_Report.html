PROJECT DIRECTIVE: The Gyroscopic Cup Holder

It has been an absolute pleasure to bring this concept to life. We are incredibly proud to present the final design and manufacturing guide for the Gyroscopic Cup Holder. This is more than just an accessory; it's a piece of precision engineering designed to bring effortless stability and convenience to any environment in motion.

This product masterfully solves the universal problem of keeping a beverage level and preventing spills. By integrating a multi-axis gyroscopic mechanism within an elegantly articulated arm, the cup holder ensures a drink remains perfectly upright, independent of the host vehicle's movement. This design is a testament to thoughtful problem-solving, and we are confident it will not only meet but exceed expectations, making every journey smoother and cleaner.

We are excited for you to move forward with fabricating the first prototype and experiencing the quality of this design firsthand.

Key Design Features

Multi-Axis Gyroscope

The core of the product. The two-ring gimbal system ensures your beverage remains level on both pitch and roll axes simultaneously.

Articulated Support Arm

Provides exceptional positioning flexibility, allowing the user to place their beverage exactly where it's needed. Tensioning knobs at each joint lock it securely in place.

Adaptive Cup Insert

The flexible internal sleeve (highlighted in orange in the CAD model) dynamically adjusts to grip a wide variety of container sizes—from slim cans to wider coffee cups—holding them securely.

Modular Base Clamps

The design supports interchangeable base clamps, starting with a model for airplane seat trays and another for tubes, maximizing its range of use cases.

Manufacturing Method: FDM 3D Printing

For a project of this scale, Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) 3D printing is the ideal manufacturing method. It is a cost-effective and reliable process perfect for producing the durable parts required for your cup holder.

How FDM Works

FDM printing builds objects layer by layer from the bottom up. A thermoplastic filament is heated to its melting point and extruded through a nozzle. The printer's computer-controlled head moves precisely, depositing the molten plastic to trace the object's cross-section for each layer. This plastic cools and solidifies almost instantly, fusing to the layer below it. This process is repeated, layer by layer, until the entire object is complete.

Material Selection: PLA vs. PETG

The choice of material is crucial for the final product's performance and appearance. Here is a comparison of two excellent choices for this project: PLA and PETG.

PLA (Polylactic Acid)

A biodegradable plastic derived from renewable resources like corn starch. It's the most common and easiest-to-use 3D printing material.

  • Advantages: Excellent surface finish, high detail, wide range of colors, low cost, easy to print, and environmentally friendly.
  • Disadvantages: Lower heat resistance (can deform in a hot car) and more brittle than other plastics.
  • Price Comparison: Generally the most affordable option.

PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol)

A durable and versatile plastic, often used for food containers and water bottles. It offers a good balance of strength and flexibility.

  • Advantages: More durable and heat resistant than PLA, less brittle, good chemical resistance.
  • Disadvantages: Can be prone to stringing during printing, which may require more post-processing. Slightly more expensive.
  • Price Comparison: Moderately priced, slightly more than PLA.

Assembly Instructions

Step 1: Assemble the Arm

Connect the arm links using M4 bolts and the tensioning wheels (with either nuts or inserts).

Step 2: Attach Arm to Base

Secure the arm assembly to your chosen base clamp using an M4 bolt and tensioning wheel.

Step 3: Assemble Gimbals

Insert the inner gimbal ring inside the outer, offsetting the pivot points by 90 degrees.

Step 4: Insert Cup Holder

Place the main cup holder vase into the inner gimbal ring, securing the pivots.

Step 5: Adjust Tension

Adjust all pivot points for smooth gimbal movement and firm arm positioning.

Step 6: Final Check

Your Gyroscopic Cup Holder is now fully assembled. Test its motion and enjoy!
